Injection Moulds

High-precision injection moulds for plastic components with complex geometries

What is Injection Moulding?

Injection moulding is a manufacturing process used to produce parts by injecting molten material into a mould. It is widely used for making plastic components with high precision and efficiency.

How Does Injection Moulding Work?

  • Material Selection – Raw plastic (e.g., PP, ABS, HDPE) is chosen based on product requirements.
  • Melting & Injection – The plastic is heated until molten and injected into a precision-designed mould.
  • Cooling & Solidification – The material cools inside the mould, taking its shape.
  • Ejection – The final product is removed from the mould and trimmed if necessary.

Advantages of Injection Moulding

  • High Precision & Consistency – Produces detailed and complex parts with accuracy.
  • Cost-Effective for Mass Production – Reduces production costs per unit.
  • Fast Production Speed – Short cycle times allow for high-volume output.
  • Strong & Durable Products – Ideal for industrial and consumer application

Die Casting Moulds

Premium die casting moulds for automotive and industrial applications

What is Die Casting?

Die casting is a metal casting process where molten metal is injected into a steel mould (die) under high pressure. It is used for creating high-precision, durable metal parts with complex shapes.

How Does Die Casting Work?

  • Molten Metal Preparation – Metals like aluminum, zinc, or magnesium are heated until molten.
  • Injection into the Mould – The molten metal is forced into a precision-engineered die under high pressure (1,500–25,000 psi).
  • Cooling & Solidification – The metal cools and hardens into the shape of the mould.
  • Ejection & Finishing – The solid part is ejected and undergoes trimming, polishing, or coating if needed.

Advantages of Die Casting

  • High Strength & Durability – Produces strong metal parts with excellent mechanical properties.
  • Precise & Complex Shapes – Can create thin-walled and intricate designs with tight tolerances.
  • Fast Production & Cost-Effective – Suitable for mass production of metal components

Cavity Moulds

Custom cavity moulds for packaging and consumer goods industries

What is a Cavity Mould?

A cavity mould is a tooling component used in injection moulding, die casting, and other moulding processes to shape materials into the desired product form. It contains hollow spaces (cavities) that define the final part's size, shape, and surface finish.

Types of Cavity Moulds

  • Single-Cavity Mould – Produces one part per cycle, ideal for custom or low-volume production.
  • Multi-Cavity Mould – Contains multiple identical cavities, increasing production efficiency.
  • Family Mould – Features different cavities in one mould to produce multiple parts at once.
  • Two-Plate & Three-Plate Moulds – Used for complex parts requiring advanced ejection systems.

How Does a Cavity Mould Work?

  • Material Injection – Molten plastic, metal, or rubber is injected into the cavity.
  • Filling & Shaping – The material takes the exact shape of the cavity under pressure.
  • Cooling & Solidification – The part cools inside the cavity, ensuring dimensional accuracy.
